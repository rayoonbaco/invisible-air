# Pass 33 — The Readable Instrument

## Purpose
Make Invisible Air easier to read without making it louder, more generic, or more dashboard-like.

## What changed
- Increased public navigation, body, evidence, and scientific text sizes.
- Strengthened contrast for explanatory and uncertainty language.
- Reduced excessive letter spacing in small labels.
- Added more breathing room around reading-room sections.
- Improved Atlas case questions, Land/Air/Time/Trust rows, readiness, provenance, and reserved-case notes.
- Improved Observation Record, Evidence Review, Source Trail, Library, and About readability.
- Increased Observatory legend, interpretation, Chapel, and field-annotation legibility while preserving the visual hierarchy.
- Added responsive adjustments so larger type remains usable on smaller screens.

## Protected
- Scientific engines and contracts.
- Observation geometry and atmosphere choreography.
- Claim boundaries, uncertainty, provenance, and human-review language.
- The Roycroft visual identity and calm exhibition character.

## Acceptance standard
A visitor should not need to lean toward the screen, decode tiny uppercase labels, or reread a scientific sentence simply because of typography.
