# Pass 36 — The Chapel of Judgment

The Chapel now turns reflection into a disciplined human judgment.

## What changed

- A single present judgment leads the room: **Hold the conclusion.**
- The judgment is placed on a quiet scale between supported, unresolved, and not supported.
- Four questions become four decision duties: what evidence permits, what it withholds, what alternative remains plausible, and what the human must do next.
- Direct doors lead to the observation record and source trail.
- The boundary explicitly refuses findings of verified transport, concentration, exposure, responsibility, or illegality.

## Scientific behavior

The judgment is derived from the live scene state. Missing inputs, unresolved terrain confidence, or fallback wind preserve an unresolved judgment. A stronger state may permit bounded interpretation, but never expands the product claim boundary.

## Design intent

The Chapel is not a verdict generator. It is the place where the instrument slows the reviewer down before inference becomes conclusion.
