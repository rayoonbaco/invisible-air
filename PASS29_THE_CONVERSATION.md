# Pass 29 — The Conversation

The Observatory now teaches the eye inside the scene. During the first minute, one quiet annotation at a time is placed beside the relevant portion of the evidence-bounded field: current wind context, the possible movement, terrain response, weakening support, and the point where human review should slow down.

These annotations are interpretations of the live scene contracts. They do not claim observation-time wind, detected methane transport, concentration, exposure, or attribution. The annotations disappear when the first-minute performance returns to silence.
