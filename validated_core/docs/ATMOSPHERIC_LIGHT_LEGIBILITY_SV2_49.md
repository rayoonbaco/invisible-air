# SV2-49 — Atmospheric Light, Shadow, and Evidence Legibility

SV2-49 coordinates restrained terrain shadow, atmospheric glow, edge separation, confidence illumination, and uncertainty luminance as a scientific legibility system.

## Purpose

The pass improves simultaneous readability of measured terrain, visual atmospheric reconstruction, confidence support, and visible uncertainty. Lighting authority is reduced by response conflict and boundary ambiguity.

## Guardrails

- Lighting does not represent methane concentration or optical density.
- Shadow does not infer plume thickness or hidden geometry.
- Glow does not certify atmospheric structure.
- Uncertainty must remain visible.
- Reviewer interaction removes cinematic emphasis.

## Scientific boundary

Light, shadow, glow, and edge contrast improve evidence legibility only. They do not measure concentration, optical density, plume thickness, distance, temperature, or atmospheric structure.
