# Pass 13 - Terrain as Narrator

## Strategic shift
The project no longer draws air as the primary object. The terrain now reveals the registered atmospheric tendency through restrained light, relief emphasis, cross-contours, pause, and loss of continuity.

## Specialist council
- Atmospheric physicist: terrain response must remain tendency, not claimed transport.
- Landscape painter: light should collect where the eye needs to understand shape.
- Cinematographer: reveal terrain in sequence, not all at once.
- Perception psychologist: source first, terrain response second, uncertainty third.
- Scientific integrity reviewer: no concentration, exposure, or measured irradiance implication.
- Minimalist sculptor: reduce luminous route language.
- Systems engineer: reuse existing registered geometry and confidence contracts.

## Changes
- Added `drawTerrainNarration()` to `static/js/plume_canvas.js`.
- Added terrain-responsive light stations along the registered field.
- Added sparse cross-contours that suggest ridges, corridors, and redirection.
- Broke continuity downstream as uncertainty rises.
- Reduced visual-symphony and segmented-route prominence.
- Preserved Pass 12 choreography and all Context/Science systems.

## Scientific boundary
Terrain illumination is a visual narrative derived from the registered path and terrain-response contracts. It is not measured light, pollutant concentration, observed transport, exposure, or verified plume geometry.

## Acceptance sentence
The viewer should feel that the land is explaining what the air may do.
