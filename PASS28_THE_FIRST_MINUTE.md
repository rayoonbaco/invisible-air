# Pass 28 — The First Minute

The Observatory's first sixty seconds are now directed as one continuous scientific exhibition.

## Sequence

1. **0–3 seconds — Stillness**: the terrain waits; the interface remains quiet.
2. **3–7 seconds — Source**: the reported source enters the instrument.
3. **7–11 seconds — Direction**: current wind gives the reconstruction its first tendency.
4. **11–18 seconds — Discovery**: the possible movement begins to reveal terrain.
5. **18–27 seconds — Terrain**: measured land visibly shapes the field.
6. **27–36 seconds — Uncertainty**: weakening support and ambiguity enter the narrative.
7. **36–46 seconds — Chapel**: the reconstruction states its boundary and invites reflection.
8. **46–57 seconds — Campus**: evidence, interpretation, and the Gatehouse become available.
9. **57–60 seconds — Silence**: narration leaves; the living instrument remains.

## Guardrail

The terrain illumination and timing are exhibition choreography. They do not represent observation time, concentration, exposure, or measured propagation speed.
